NITARP (NASA/IPAC Teacher Archive Research Program)

NITARP (NASA/IPAC Teacher Archive Research Program) is a NASA research program that partners small groups of high school educators and their students with a mentor professional astronomer for an original research project. At the end, each team writes up the results of their research and presents it in a science poster session at an American Astronomical Society meeting.

My dad, a high school earth and space science teacher, was one of the teachers selected for this project. The research project that we worked on was OIRMA (Optical/Infrared Mapping of AGN). AGN (Active Galactic Nuclei) are supermassive black holes that are found at the center of some galaxies. They are surrounded by a rotating accretion disc, which is then surrounded by a thick, dusty, donut-shaped torus. The goal of the project was to use continuum reverberation mapping (CRM) to measure the size of their accretion disks. Over the course of the project, I created and analyzed over 250 light curves to help accomplish this goal. The project team also ended up making four posters, as shown below.
