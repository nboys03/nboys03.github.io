Classes

Throughout my time at UW-Madison, I’ve had the opportunity to take a variety of courses that have not only expanded my knowledge of but have also fueled my passion for mechanical engineering. Below is a list of a few of my favorite courses that have challenged me, fostered my curiosity for engineering, and contributed to my growth as an engineering student!

ME 201: Introduction to Mechanical Engineering

This was one of the first classes that I’ve taken at UW-Madison, and it did a fantastic job of introducing the engineering design process. It had a semester-long project which main involved the design of an autonomous trolley. There were many challenges that the trolley was put through including a hill climb, a race, and following a leading trolley that randomly sped up and down. Some fun features of the trolley were a spoiler, custom hubcaps, brake lights, and an airbrake. Below are some pictures of the trolley with the 3D printed parts that I designed for it, as well as a video about the trolley.

ME 306/307: Mechanics of Materials

I greatly enjoyed Mechanics of Materials because it allowed me to understand more of the theory behind materials, stresses, and failure. It did a fantastic job applying these concepts to real-world problems. This class also included a lab section that further cemented these ideas and let me control real-life failure experiments.

ME 361: Thermodynamics

Despite being concerned about this course's difficulty before I took it, it turned out to be one of my my favorite classes. Professor Nellis did a fantastic job clearly explaining the topics, and I enjoyed learning more about how the heat and energy systems work in real life.

ME 310: Polymers

For better or worse, polymers and plastics are everywhere. I enjoyed Polymers Processing and Manufacturing because it provided an interesting look into how so many products are made. It combined theory with hands-on learning, which gave me the ability to understand the theory and function of many processing techniques like injection molding, extrusion, thermoforming, and additive manufacturing. Below is one of the models that I made in this course: an aerial view of the UW-Madison college of engineering campus.

EMA 202: Dynamics

Kinematics is by far my favorite branch of physics, and dynamics gave me a deeper understanding of this topic. Professor Sonny did a fantastic job teaching this topic in a clear way. Prior to this class, I had only ever solved kinematics problems using algebra, so it was fun to learn how to use calculus to solve real-world physics problems.

Comp Sci 200, 300, and 400: Java I-III

While I love mechanical engineering, I also love coding. Comp Sci 200 gave a great introduction to Java and its syntax. Comp Sci 300 went more in-depth and introduced me to sorting algorithms and algorithm efficiency. Comp Sci 400 introduced me to data structures, webpage/app development, and how to design programs efficiently.
